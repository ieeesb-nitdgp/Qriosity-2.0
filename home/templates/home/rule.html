  • Rules & Regulations:

  • Round 1 consists of a total of 10 questions carrying 15 points each.
  • Upon providing answer to all of the questions in round 1, 10 algorithms will be revealed. Answers of the questions are to be used to solve the algorithms.
  • Solving one algorithm will yield a letter of the English alphabet.
  • On solving the algorithms of round 1, all the letters so obtained can be used to generate the passcode, which have a bonus point of 60 associated with it. The rankings on the leaderboard will determine who gets to participate in round 2.
  • Round 2 carries 5 questions carrying 20 points each .
  • Questions of round 1 will contain hints. Requesting for hints will result in the lowering of points obtainable from that particular question by 5.
  • Questions of round 2 will not contain any hints.
  • Answers to the questions have to be given in a format that shall not contain any spaces and special characters to separate words and shall all be in lower case . For example to answer for m-rna you must write mrna.
  • If the question asks for three values ,the answer must be specified in proper sequence of the variables without space.
  • The one who has the maximum points at the end of the quiz will be the winner.
  • In case of a tie, the earlier the submission, the higher the ranking.
  • To save time, participants are advised to keep noting the answers of each question of first round in a notebook for generation of final passcode . In case the participant forgets the answers to the questions of first round while generating the passcode, the option to view all answers entered shall be provided.
